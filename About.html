About

Early Life

Aubrey "Drake" Graham was born on October 24, 1986, in Toronto, Ontario. His father, Dennis Graham, is an African American and a practicing Catholic from Memphis, Tennessee, and worked as a drummer, performing alongside country musician Jerry Lee Lewis. Drake's mother, Sandra "Sandi" Graham (n√©e Sher), is an Ashkenazi Jewish Canadian who worked as an English teacher and florist. His parents met after Dennis performed at a place called "Club Bluenote" in Toronto, where he first interacted with Sandra, who was in attendance. He is a dual citizen of the United States and Canada. In his youth, Drake attended a Jewish day school and formally celebrated becoming a Bar Mitzvah in a religious service.

Drake was raised in two Toronto neighborhoods. He lived on Weston Road in the city's working-class west end until grade six, playing minor hockey. He then moved to one of the city's affluent neighborhoods, Forest Hill, in 2000.

Career

At 15, wanting to be an actor, a high school friend introduced Drake to his father, who was an acting agent. The agent found Drake a role in Canadian teen drama series Degrassi: The Next Generation. Drake portrayed Jimmy Brooks, a basketball star who became physically disabled after he was shot by a classmate.

After becoming musically inspired by Jay Z and Clipse, Drake released his debut mixtape, Room for Improvement, in 2006. In 2007, he released his second mixtape, Comeback Season. In 2009, Drake released his third mixtape, So Far Gone. Due to the success of the mixtape, Drake was the subject of a bidding war from various labels, often reported as "one of the biggest bidding wars ever". It was confirmed later Drake signed a recording contract with Young Money Entertainment.

On June 15th, 2010 Drake released his debut album Thank Me Later, debuting at number one on the Billboard 200. Since then Drake has gone on to release four more albums, two compilation albums, two extended plays, and three mixtapes. He has become a world sensation and now one of the most known hip hop/rap artists in the world.
